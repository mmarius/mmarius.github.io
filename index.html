Marius Mosbach

I am a postdoctoral fellow at McGill University and a researcher at Mila - Quebec AI Institute in Montréal .

Before that, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Language Science and Technology Department of Saarland University , where I also did my PhD in Computer Science. My PhD advisor was Dietrich Klakow.

The overarching goal of my research is to build natural language processing (NLP) systems that are well understood, robust, and adaptable; we should have a rigorous understanding of when and why NLP systems work or fail, they should be robust in a variety of real-world scenarios with guarantees about their performance and generalization, and they should be easy to adapt to new domains and problems.

For our work, my collaborators and I have received a Best Paper Award at COLING 2022, the Best Theme Paper Award at ACL 2023, and the Most Interesting Paper Award at the BabyLM Challenge 2023 .

In my free time, I enjoy CrossFit , playing soccer , and baking .
